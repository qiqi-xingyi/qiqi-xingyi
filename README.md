## About Me

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at **Kent State University**, jointly affiliated with the **Cleveland Clinic**, advised by **Prof. Qiang Guan** and co-advised by **Dr. Feixiong Cheng**.

My research lies at the intersection of **quantum computing**, **high-performance computing**, and **computational biology**. I develop quantum–classical methods and systems for biological structure modeling and drug discovery—from problem formulation and quantum algorithms to utility-scale hardware execution and scientific validation.

### Research Focus

- **Hardware-Executed Quantum Biocomputing** — Biological structure workflows designed for and executed on real quantum processors.
- **Quantum Drug Discovery** — Quantum methods for protein conformations, binding pockets, docking, and ligand response.
- **Quantum–HPC Co-design** — Scalable integration of quantum execution, HPC, runtime systems, optimization, and scientific data pipelines.
- **Quantum Bioinformatics** — Quantum-aware representations of biological sequence, structure, conformational states, and molecular interactions.
